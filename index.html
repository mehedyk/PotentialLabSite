About the Lab

The Artificial Intelligence Lab (AI Lab) was established in Spring 2025, initiated by Sudipto Pramanik, Lecturer, Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Daffodil International University. The lab’s mission is to conduct high-impact research in the fields of Deep Learning, Natural Language Processing, and Signal Processing while nurturing the next generation of researchers. We aim to build a vibrant research ecosystem within the campus where students actively engage in hands-on research, publish their work in reputed venues, and explore real-world AI applications. Our vision is to foster innovation, curiosity, and interdisciplinary collaboration—empowering students and faculty to create cutting-edge AI solutions that contribute to both technological progress and societal benefit.

Research Areas

Cutting-edge research across multiple domains of artificial intelligence and machine learning

Large Language Models

Tuning and deploying LLMs like GPT, LLaMA, and Mistral for domain-specific applications including email generation, text summarization, and Bengali NLP.

Deep Learning

Advanced neural network architectures for computer vision, natural language understanding, and multimodal AI systems with applications in healthcare and automation.

Reinforcement Learning

Developing intelligent agents using RL techniques including Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) and policy gradient methods for sequential decision-making tasks.

Signal Processing

Digital signal processing for biomedical applications, ECG/PPG analysis, and real-time health monitoring systems.

Explainable AI

Building interpretable machine learning models with transparency and trust, focusing on understanding AI decision-making processes and model behavior.

Publications, Projects & Contributions

Enhancing Genre Fidelity in Children’s Story Generation via Fine-Tuned LLMs

doi: 10.1109/QPAIN66474.2025.11171897

Journal: 2025 International Conference on Quantum Photonics, Artificial Intelligence, and Networking (QPAIN)

The increasing demand for genre-specific creative writing, particularly in children’s storytelling, has revealed significant limitations in the controllability and coherence of large language models (LLMs) during story generation. While models such as LLaMA-2 are capable of generating fluent narratives, they often fail to consistently align them with specific genre prompts, reducing their thematic consistency. This paper addresses the critical challenge of enhancing genre fidelity in children’s story generation by presenting a genre-conditioned story generation framework that employs a fine-tuned LLaMA- 2 7B model, trained on a curated, multi-genre children’s story dataset. To evaluate the genre alignment, we utilize a RoBERTa- based classifier trained for multi-class genre classification across key genres in children’s literature such as Horror, Science Fiction, Humor & Comedy, and Mystery & Detective. Comparative analysis of stories generated by both the pretrained and fine-tuned models demonstrates that the fine-tuned model significantly improves genre controllability, outperforming the pretrained model. By improving genre fidelity, this work enhances the ability of LLMs to generate more thematically consistent and engaging children’s stories, supporting the development of controlled, genre-aware LLMs for creative writing applications.

LoRGen: A Two-Stage LLM Framework for Human-Aligned LoR Generation via Efficient Fine-Tuning and Direct Preference Optimization.

Status: Under Review

Journal: IEEE Access

This paper presents LoRGen, a two-stage frame- work for enhancing Large Language Model (LLM)-based Letter of Recommendation (LoR) generation through efficient fine- tuning and Direct Preference Optimization (DPO). Automation of LoR writing with generative models is crucial, as it reduces the workload of bulk recommendation writing, making the process less labor-intensive, more time-efficient, and standardized. Over the years, fine-tuned LLMs have been widely employed for downstream task-specific text generation, where these models have consistently demonstrated strong performance. However, in the context of LoR generation, fine-tuning remains inadequate to maintain coherence, context alignment, human-likeness, and personalization as required. To address these limitations, in our approach, supervised fine-tuning (SFT) has been integrated with DPO in a two-stage pipeline. In the first stage, the LLaMA-3.2- 1B model is fine-tuned on a custom dataset of LoR prompts and sample responses using parameter-efficient Low-Rank Adapta- tion (LoRA). In the second stage, DPO is employed to incorporate human preferences, thereby optimizing the tone, clarity, and per- sonalization of the generated LoRs. Evaluation through ROUGE scores and qualitative human analysis demonstrates significant improvements in DPO-trained models over the SFT baselines. This work therefore highlights the effectiveness of systematically applying DPO to recommendation letter generation and estab- lishes its potential as a scalable approach for human-aligned text generation in academic and professional domains.

Enhancing Emotion Classification with Interpretability using LoRA-Finetuned LLaMA-3.2-3B

Status: Under Review

Journal: 11th IEEE International Women in Engineering (WIE) Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering (WIECON-ECE)

Understanding human emotions through language is a fundamental aspect of building empathetic and trustworthy AI systems. While traditional emotion classification models can detect basic emotional states from text, they typically lack the ability to explain their predictions—making them less transparent and harder to trust in real-world applications. In this work, we address this gap by supervised fine-tuning Meta’s LLaMA-3.2 3B-Instruct model using Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) on a custom dataset that pairs user comments with both emotion labels and natural language explanations. Our approach enables the model not only to classify the underlying emotion in a given text but also to generate a coherent justification for its prediction. Importantly, our results show that the model maintains strong classification accuracy even while generating detailed explanations, which supports the practical adoption of such explainable models. Furthermore, we develop an interactive Gradio-based user interface that enables users to input text and perform emotion classification with explanations. Side-by-side comparisons between the pretrained and fine-tuned models were also performed to demonstrate the contributions of the fine-tuning approach. This work, therefore, highlights the potential of lightweight fine-tuning techniques to enhance the reasoning capabilities of large language models in emotionally intelligent applications, such as mental health monitoring, customer sentiment analysis, and human-computer interaction.
